Work

WHAT WE DO

Blended audio tutoring, in mother tongue languages, with digital tools allowing people to learn independently and at their own pace, anywhere, anytime. This allows students to progress to new levels of learning as soon as they demonstrate mastery of a topic, rather than moving forward based on the number of hours spent in a classroom. Add to this a flexible coaching and mentoring program that is accessible 24 hours, 7 days a week, 365 days a year, and you enable customized pathways to achievement, rapid skills transfer and linkages to opportunities.

ACHIEVING OUR VISION

We focus on the common goal of empowering communities through education and skills training by harnessing innovation.

This means that we use technology to optimally enable sustainable access to learning, without compromising human interactions.

THE CHALLENGE

The break from poverty’s grip becomes believable when we see the next generation having access to quality contextual education.

The stakes are high. Limited opportunity for too many young people results in dramatically lower life prospects for them and a worse quality of life for all of us.

    Community Safety

    What does it mean to be a neighbourhood
    watch member and active citizen?

    • Being a member of a Neighbourhood Watch does not give a person any special rights or privileges. It means that you have extra responsibility to be a good role model and a responsible and law-abiding citizen.
    • An active citizen can take part in delivering services which are needed in practical ways such as voicing your opinions and participating in the fight against crime.
    • You can prevent crime by ensuring your own safety, e.g. not walking alone at night in areas that are not well-lit.
    • You can also help to prevent crime by being observant and reporting any suspicious looking persons or activities to SAPS.
    • If all citizens in a community stand together, crime will drop, and we will have healthier and happier communities with fewer incidences of violence or xenophobia.

    What are my rights?

    All the rights and responsibilities can be found in the constitution of South Africa. It applies to all citizens and residents equally. Note that even if someone is only visiting as a tourist it also applies to them.

    It is important that we read and understand the constitution of South Africa and the associated human rights it supports. Read the following extracts and summaries from the constitution.

    Read each section carefully and then write notes in response to the questions or prompts in the space allowed below each section.

    Community Safety

    From Chapter 2 of the Constitution – Human Rights

    Section 12 (1) – Freedom and security of the person

    Everyone has the right to freedom and security of the person, which includes the right

    1. Not to be deprived of freedom arbitrarily or without just cause;
    2. Not to be detained without trial;
    3. To be free from all forms of violence from either public or private sources;
    4. Not to be tortured in any way; and
    5. Not to be treated or punished in a cruel, inhuman or degrading way.

    Do you feel that you and your family are free and secure in the place where you live?

    Think of practical ways that you could improve your community that would help everybody feel that they are free and secure in their daily lives.
